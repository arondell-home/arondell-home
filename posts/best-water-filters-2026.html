2. Brita ‚Äî Best Budget Option

Brita is the Honda Civic of water filters. It's everywhere, it's cheap, and it does the basics. The Standard filter handles chlorine taste and odor, and the newer Elite filter adds lead reduction. At ~$0.18/gallon, it's the most affordable option by far.

But Brita doesn't remove PFAS, most heavy metals (without the Elite filter), or the majority of dissolved solids. If your tap water is already decent, Brita is fine. If you're worried about contaminants, look elsewhere.

3. PUR ‚Äî Best Mid-Range

PUR sits between Brita and ZeroWater in both price and performance. The Plus filters add lead reduction and remove slightly more contaminants than Brita Standard. The filter indicator light is handy. But like Brita, it doesn't remove PFAS or most dissolved solids.

4. Clearly Filtered ‚Äî Best Premium Alternative

Clearly Filtered is the sleeper pick. Their 3-stage filter removes over 365 contaminants including PFAS, lead, and fluoride. With 100-gallon filter life, the cost per gallon is actually competitive with ZeroWater. The pitcher itself is pricier upfront (~$80), but it's a well-made product.

The downside: no TDS meter, less brand recognition, and harder to find replacement filters locally.

The Verdict

For clean water on any budget: ZeroWater. The 000 TDS reading and included meter make it the most transparent and effective pitcher filter. Yes, filters cost more ‚Äî but you're paying for actual filtration, not marketing.

For taste improvement only: Brita. It's cheap, easy, and does the basics. No shame in it.

For maximum contaminant removal + filter life: Clearly Filtered. Premium price, premium performance, longer filter life offsets the cost.
